## Overview

This platform supported multiple enterprise domains for MODEC, including performance management, project management,
and digital operational visibility. The challenge was not just building features. It was designing an architecture that
allowed distinct modules to evolve independently while still working as one enterprise product.

## Problem

The system had to serve multiple domains with different operational needs:

- Performance Management
- Project Management
- Digital Control Tower

The Digital Control Tower was especially complex because it handled the EPCI lifecycle of offshore platforms and needed
to connect project and operational views in a meaningful way.

## Architecture

The architectural direction centered on decomposition:

- microservices were used to separate backend capabilities by domain and ownership boundaries
- microfrontends enabled teams to evolve major modules with less coupling in the UI layer
- domain-driven module ownership made it easier to isolate business logic and release changes incrementally
- shared integration layers kept cross-module visibility possible without rebuilding the system as a single unit

This was less about trendy architecture and more about giving a large enterprise system room to scale without
collapsing into central coordination bottlenecks.

## Technical Challenges

- separating modules without breaking enterprise-level workflows
- integrating operational and project data for offshore lifecycle visibility
- coordinating architectural decisions across internal teams, MODEC stakeholders, and consultancies
- keeping the system understandable while increasing its modularity

## Solution

The platform was evolved with explicit module ownership:

- Performance module
- Projects module
- Digital Control Tower

The Digital Control Tower required careful data integration design because the EPCI lifecycle crosses planning,
execution, and operational visibility concerns. The solution was to define backend and frontend boundaries that mapped
to business capabilities while preserving the ability to compose information across modules.

## Technologies Used

- C#
- .NET
- Microservices
- Microfrontends
- Enterprise platform integration patterns

## Impact

The platform became more scalable organizationally and technically. Teams could move faster within clearer module
boundaries, while the enterprise product remained coherent enough to support MODEC's complex operational environment.
