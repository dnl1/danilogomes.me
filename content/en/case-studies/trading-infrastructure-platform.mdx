## Overview

This work focused on the foundation of trading platform infrastructure in a brokerage environment where reliability,
latency, and protocol correctness directly affect business operations. The system needed to support trading workflows,
market connectivity, and internal service coordination without introducing instability into critical order flows.

## Problem

Brokerage systems are difficult because they sit at the intersection of market protocol requirements, internal business
rules, and operational resilience. The platform needed to coordinate multiple internal services while maintaining low
latency, predictable messaging behavior, and safe failure handling under financial workloads.

## Architecture

The platform relied on a protocol-aware, message-driven architecture:

- FIX was used for trading-oriented interactions where protocol semantics and message sequencing mattered.
- RabbitMQ supported asynchronous communication between internal services and workflow boundaries.
- gRPC and RPC were used for lower-latency synchronous communication paths where direct service contracts were needed.
- Proprietary low-level libraries handled specialized integration points close to the trading core.

This architecture separated transport concerns by use case instead of forcing all workloads into a single integration
model.

## Technical Challenges

- Balancing low-latency communication with operational reliability in a financial environment
- Handling message ordering, retries, and protocol expectations without compromising trading behavior
- Integrating synchronous and asynchronous patterns across services with different latency and consistency needs
- Operating close to low-level libraries where abstraction layers were intentionally thin

## Solution

The engineering approach emphasized clear communication boundaries:

- Use message queues where decoupling and resilience mattered more than immediate response times
- Use direct RPC-style communication where low overhead and contract clarity were required
- Keep protocol-sensitive trading flows close to dedicated integration layers instead of over-generalizing them
- Design around failure handling, observability, and operational predictability from the start

Rather than treating messaging as a generic infrastructure concern, the platform used protocol and transport choices as
part of the system design itself.

## Technologies Used

- FIX protocol
- RabbitMQ
- gRPC
- RPC
- Proprietary low-level integration libraries

## Impact

This work helped create the infrastructure foundation for a trading platform where communication patterns, reliability,
and protocol correctness were part of the product itself. The result was a system shape better aligned with brokerage
operations and the realities of financial messaging workloads.
