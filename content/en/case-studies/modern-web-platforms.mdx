## Overview

This work focused on Orchid JMS, a journey management platform for surrogacy, egg donation, and sperm donation
agencies. The product had to support the full lifecycle of third-party journeys, from first contact through birth and
beyond, while remaining configurable enough for agencies with different processes and operating models.

## Problem

Agencies in this space often rely on email, spreadsheets, and chat-based coordination for intake, screening, matching,
and case management. That creates predictable failure modes:

- fragmented visibility across active journeys
- manual coordination between teams
- inconsistent process execution
- difficulty adapting the system to agency-specific workflows
- weak reporting on productivity, pipeline, and case progression

The challenge was to build a product that felt operationally rigorous without becoming rigid.

## Architecture

The architecture combined:

- Next.js for the operational frontend and workflow-heavy internal views
- TypeScript for maintainable UI logic and safer product iteration
- Vercel for fast deployment, preview environments, and frontend delivery
- configurable workflow and data modeling to support agency-specific intake, screening, matching, and case structures

The system was intentionally API-driven, allowing workflow execution, case visibility, reporting, and custom data
collection to evolve without tightly coupling the product to a single agency model.

## Technical Challenges

- modeling workflows that were configurable without becoming chaotic
- supporting custom data fields and task-oriented processes across agencies
- keeping case management usable while the domain remained operationally dense
- building reporting surfaces that could extract meaningful metrics from day-to-day operations
- balancing product flexibility with a maintainable architecture

## Solution

The solution was a pragmatic full-stack operational platform:

- centralize intake, screening, matching, and case management into one system
- structure the product around task-oriented workflows that reduce mistakes and missing steps
- support agency-specific customization through configurable data fields, forms, templates, and process flows
- provide operational reporting that helps teams understand caseload, conversion, matching times, and revenue signals

This approach let the product stay flexible for different agencies while preserving a cleaner engineering model behind
the scenes.

## Technologies Used

- Next.js
- TypeScript
- Vercel
- Workflow systems
- Reporting and metrics

## Impact

Orchid JMS improved process visibility, reduced administrative overhead, and gave agencies a stronger operational system
for managing journeys end to end. From an engineering perspective, it showed how configurable workflow software can be
built without sacrificing clarity, maintainability, and delivery speed.
